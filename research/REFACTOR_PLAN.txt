## Next Step: Checkpoint / Resume For Full-Convergence Demos

With the 230× speedup, 20 live iterations (~20 s) is enough for class to see the contraction rate. Actually reaching `tol=1e-2` requires ~112 iterations (~2 min at 1 s/iter). For a more impressive end-to-end demo, checkpoint/resume can pre-run a long trace and resume live.

- `run_socp_quantum_ipm` gets optional `init_state=None` and `checkpoint_path=None`.
- Cell 46 loads a checkpoint if present and prints `Resuming from N prior iterations, gap=X.XXX`.
- Instructor runs `max_iter=200` offline and commits `qipm_checkpoint.npz`.
- In class, the notebook loads the checkpoint, runs 5 live iterations, and shows the full convergence history.
- Notebook remains self-contained: if the `.npz` is missing, it starts fresh with Phase-I.

Pedagogical value: "Quantum HPC" framing — quantum compute jobs run offline, results resume anywhere. Also demonstrates that the algorithm really does converge, not just the first 20 steps.
